---
title: Recording Screen & Camera
description: Capture your screen, camera, and audio with Voom's native macOS recorder
---

Voom captures your screen at full Retina resolution using ScreenCaptureKit, with optional camera overlay and dual audio sources.

## Starting a Recording

You can start recording in multiple ways:

<Steps>
  <Step title="From the menu bar">
    Click the Voom icon in your menu bar, configure your sources, then click **Start Recording**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="With keyboard shortcut">
    Press `Cmd+Shift+R` from anywhere to instantly start/stop recording
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The global keyboard shortcut can be disabled in Settings → Keyboard if you prefer manual control.
</Note>

## Recording Sources

Voom supports three capture sources that can be combined:

### Screen Capture

Records your selected display at native Retina resolution using HEVC hardware encoding:

- Captures at full backing scale factor (2x for Retina displays)
- 60 FPS frame rate
- HEVC/H.265 compression via VideoToolbox
- Cursor always visible in recordings
- Voom's own windows are automatically excluded (except camera overlay)

<Tip>
  Select your display from the dropdown before recording. Voom remembers your last choice.
</Tip>

### Camera Overlay

Adds a picture-in-picture webcam feed to your recording:

- Captures at 720p60 from your default camera
- Draggable positioning (bottom-left, bottom-right, top-left, top-right)
- The camera overlay is captured directly by ScreenCaptureKit—what you see is what gets recorded
- Camera feed is optional and can be toggled on/off per recording

<Warning>
  The camera overlay appears as a floating window. Position it before recording starts, as the position is captured into the final video.
</Warning>

### Audio Recording

Voom can capture two audio sources simultaneously:

**System Audio**
- Records all system sound (apps, alerts, browser audio)
- Captured at 48kHz stereo
- Requires Screen Recording permission

**Microphone Audio**
- Records from your default microphone
- Captured separately from system audio for better quality
- Mixed into the final video alongside system audio

<Note>
  Both audio sources are mixed into the final video file. Transcription uses the combined audio track.
</Note>

## During Recording

### Recording Controls

While recording, you'll see:

- **Live timer** showing elapsed recording time (MM:SS format)
- **Pause/Resume** button to temporarily pause capture
- **Stop** button to end the recording

### Pause & Resume

Pausing a recording freezes all capture:

- Screen frames stop being captured
- Audio (system + mic) stops recording
- Timer pauses
- Resume picks up seamlessly—paused time is removed from the final video

<Tip>
  Pause is useful for handling interruptions without restarting your recording.
</Tip>

### Stopping Recording

When you stop:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Video finalization">
    Voom finalizes the HEVC video file and generates a thumbnail
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Auto-save to library">
    Recording is saved to `~/Movies/Voom/` and appears in your library immediately
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Auto-transcription (if enabled)">
    If your recording has audio and auto-transcribe is enabled, WhisperKit begins transcribing in the background
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recording Settings

### Auto-Transcription

By default, recordings with audio are automatically transcribed after capture:

- Enabled: Settings → General → "Automatically transcribe recordings"
- Uses WhisperKit's distil-large-v3 model
- Runs entirely on-device (nothing leaves your Mac)
- Also generates AI title and summary (if Apple Intelligence is available)

### Global Hotkey

Toggle the `Cmd+Shift+R` shortcut:

- Enabled: Settings → Keyboard → "Global keyboard shortcut"
- Works from any app, even when Voom is hidden
- Starts recording if idle, stops if already recording

## Output Format

Recordings are saved as `.mov` files with:

- **Video codec:** HEVC (H.265) hardware-accelerated
- **Resolution:** Native display resolution (e.g., 2880×1800 for Retina 13")
- **Frame rate:** 60 FPS
- **Audio codec:** AAC, 48kHz stereo (system + mic mixed)
- **File size:** Approximately 50-150 MB per minute (depends on content)

## Permissions Required

<Warning>
  Voom requires the following macOS permissions. You'll be prompted on first use:
</Warning>

- **Screen Recording** — Required for capturing screen and system audio
- **Camera** — Required if using camera overlay
- **Microphone** — Required if recording microphone audio

Grant permissions in **System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording / Camera / Microphone**.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="No displays appear in the dropdown">
    Restart Voom after granting Screen Recording permission. macOS sometimes requires an app restart for ScreenCaptureKit to detect displays.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Camera overlay doesn't appear in recording">
    The camera overlay window must be visible on-screen during recording. If it's minimized or hidden, it won't be captured. Reposition it before starting.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="System audio isn't captured">
    Check that:
    - Screen Recording permission is granted in System Settings
    - "System Audio" toggle is enabled in the Voom recording panel
    - You're playing audio from an app (some apps route audio differently)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Recording starts but immediately stops">
    This usually indicates a codec error. Check Console.app for "Voom" logs. Requires macOS 15+ with HEVC VideoToolbox support.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
